# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People in Amman and Jordan who want to discover, trust, visit, or order from Midyaji Basha. The site serves Arabic and English visitors, including shoppers nearby, Turkish street food fans, seafood seekers, and people using Google or AI assistants to ask direct questions about mussels, Midye Dolma, Fatteh Yalanji, and Cik Kofte.

## Product Purpose

Midyaji Basha is a bilingual restaurant brand website for a Turkish Street Food Restaurant in Amman, Jordan. Its purpose is to make the business discoverable, credible, and easy to contact while positioning mussels as the core entity and Midye Dolma as the signature Turkish mussel dish.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic, warm, crafted. The site should feel premium and food-led, with Turkish street-food energy translated into a polished Amman restaurant presence.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic delivery-app styling, fake luxury, stock-looking restaurant templates, SEO text dumps, unverified business claims, fake ratings, invented opening hours, and visual changes that obscure the existing bilingual routing or brand assets.

## Design Principles

Preserve the existing identity before adding polish.
Make food and place feel real through imagery, specific language, and verified contact paths.
Answer search and AI questions directly while keeping copy human and readable.
Treat Arabic as a first-class experience, not a translated afterthought.
Use motion and glow as premium feedback, not decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Maintain readable contrast on dark imagery and dark surfaces. Support reduced motion where animation is used. Keep keyboard-visible interactions, semantic headings, clear link labels, and correct RTL layout for Arabic users.
